Description

Designed by architect Norte Júnior in 1904/05, the house was built on the orders of the painter José Malhoa, to serve both as his residence and studio. The first artist’s house of the capital, Valmor Prize in 1905, it was integrated in the urban growth plan of the city of Lisbon. In 1932, the “House of Malhoa” was purchased by doctor and art collector António Anastácio Gonçalves to serve as his residence but also with the intention of later becoming a public museum.
